ABSTRACT: 
The organic and isotopic geochemical study of two sediment cores 
(KH-79-3, L-3, and KH-79-3, C-3) from the Oki Ridge in the Japan Sea 
has revealed that total organic carbon (TOC) mass accumulation rates 
are extremely high in the 12-11 ka (calendar age) interval and TOC in 
the sections in the 24-17 ka interval is depleted in 13C by 3.5 per mil 
relative to Holocene sediments. Alkenone sea surface temperature (SST) 
shows a decrease from 18� to 14�C from 17.5 to 11.6 ka and a sharp 
increase from 14� to 19�C from 11.6 to 11.1 ka. The SST changes are 
associated with the inflow of cold seawater with the vertical water 
mixing and the inflow of warm Tsushima Current into the Japan Sea. 
The d13C values for both 24-methylcholesta-5,22-dien-3B-ol (diatom 
marker) and dinosterol (dinoflagellate marker), are at their minimum 
from 24 to 17 ka, while those for long-chain alkenones are not. 
The theoretical considerations on d13C for biomarkers suggest 
low photosynthetic carbon demand of diatoms and dinoflagellates 
from 24 to 17 ka.
